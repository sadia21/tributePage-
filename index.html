Michelle Obama

United states 44th first lady (2009-2017) Michelle Obama

First Lady Michelle LaVaughn Robinson Obama is a lawyer, writer, and the wife of the 44th President, Barack Obama. She is the first African-American First Lady of the United States. Through her four main initiatives, she became a role model for women and an advocate for healthy families, service members and their families, higher education, and international adolescent girls education.

Important acomplishments of Michelle Obama

Before becoming first lady

  • Michelle Robinson served as an assistant to the mayor of Chicago
  • She was the founding executive director of Chicago chapter of public allies
  • She served as associate dean of student services for the university of Chicago
  • She served as vice president for community and external affairs at Uchicago medicine

As first lady

  • Michelle Obama became the first African American first lady of the United States
  • She launched a series of successful national campaigns as first lady
  • She became a role model who would positively influence the world view of African Americans

Other notable achievement and honors

  • Her memoir “Becoming” was one of the bestselling books of the decade in 2019
  • She won the 2020 Grammy award for best spoken word album
  • Michelle Obama was named 2008 TIME Woman of The Year in 2019
